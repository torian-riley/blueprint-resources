SDR Tech Stack Recommendations

A Blueprint-curated toolkit for early-stage SaaS companies building an outbound sales development program from the ground up.

1

CRM  Recommended: HubSpot

HubSpot is the CRM of choice across the Blueprint portfolio. Relative to alternatives, it stands out on price, customer support quality, and an interface that sales teams actually adopt without much hand-holding. The Smart CRM is free and works fine for basic contact and deal tracking, but most teams will want to layer in one or more Hubs as they build out their GTM motion.

Core hubs we recommend

Sales Hub

Pipeline management, deal tracking, sequences, meeting scheduling, and rep activity reporting. The primary tool for your SDRs and AEs to manage outbound and close deals.

Marketing Hub

Email marketing, landing pages, ad tracking, lead nurturing workflows, and marketing attribution. Powers your demand-gen and inbound motion alongside outbound.

Content Hub

CMS, blog, SEO tools, and AI content generation. Relevant once your team is building a content engine to support inbound pipeline.

Also available

Service Hub

Ticketing, knowledge base, and CS workflows. Typically added once the core sales and marketing motion is running.

Commerce Hub

CPQ, billing, and payments. Useful if you want to manage quotes and collect payment directly within HubSpot.

Data Hub

Data sync, programmable automation, and data quality tooling. Most relevant once HubSpot is your system of record and you need cross-platform data integrity.

Dialer  Recommended: Nooks

For most portcos building an outbound motion from scratch, we recommend Nooks. It bundles a parallel dialer, real-time AI call coaching, post-call analytics, and signal-based prospect prioritization into a single workspace, which means your SDRs are not context-switching between tools to research, dial, and log calls.

That said, Nooks and Orum are genuinely close in core functionality. Our recommendation is based on the overall workflow experience and breadth of features rather than a meaningful gap between the two. If your team is already on Orum or has a strong preference for it, that is a reasonable choice.

Nooks vs. Orum vs. Gong

Note: Gong is a conversation intelligence and revenue platform, not a parallel dialer. It is included here because teams often evaluate it alongside dialers when thinking about call coaching and pipeline analytics. Dialers are pre-pipeline tools (more conversations booked), while Gong operates mid-to-post pipeline (better conversations closed). Gong starts making sense once your team is running enough sales calls that coaching at scale becomes a bottleneck, typically 3+ quota-carrying reps with meaningful call volume.

Data Enrichment  Recommended: Clay

Clay is a no-code enrichment platform that aggregates 150+ data providers into a single waterfall workflow. Instead of subscribing to Apollo, ZoomInfo, and Clearbit separately, you build one Clay table that queries all of them in sequence, only paying for what returns a result. For building SDR lists and enriching contacts at scale, it is the most efficient tool on the market.

Example workflow: Pull a list of 500 companies matching your ICP, find the VP of Sales at each, enrich with verified email, direct dial, and LinkedIn URL, then push directly into HubSpot and your sequencing tool, fully automated with no manual research.

Understanding Clay credits

Clay uses a credit-based system. Each data action (a lookup, enrichment, or AI step) consumes credits. Credits are not charged on failed lookups. Here is a rough guide to what common SDR workflows cost:

Company enrichment

~6–10 credits

Per company. Covers firmographics, technographics, headcount, funding stage, and industry. Good starting point before identifying contacts.

Contact identification

~2–5 credits

Per contact. Finding a decision-maker by title (e.g., "VP of Sales") from Clay's database of people records.

Email enrichment

~3–6 credits

Per contact. Waterfall across multiple providers to find and verify a professional email address.

Phone enrichment

~5–13 credits

Per contact. Direct dials are harder to source and cost more. Budget on the higher end for mobile numbers.

Full contact build

~15–35 credits

Per contact end-to-end. Includes company enrichment, contact ID, email, phone, and LinkedIn. Budget roughly 25 credits/contact for planning.

AI personalization

~5–25 credits

Per action. Claygent research tasks like writing a custom first line or summarizing a prospect's LinkedIn vary by model and complexity.

4

Sequencing  Recommended: Outreach

OTR

Outreach

The industry-standard sales engagement platform for running multi-channel outbound sequences (email, phone, LinkedIn, SMS). Outreach sits on top of your CRM and gives SDRs a structured daily task queue, pulling in contacts, automating follow-ups, and logging all activity back to HubSpot. Purpose-built for high-velocity outbound, with strong A/B testing, reply detection, and sequence analytics. Integrates natively with HubSpot, Nooks, Clay, and Gong.

outreach.io ↗
Note on minimum spend. Outreach requires a minimum annual commitment (typically $10,000+ before per-seat costs) that can be prohibitive for very early-stage teams. If your team has fewer than 2–3 SDRs or is not ready for that level of investment, HubSpot's native sequences are a viable starting point and can handle the basics until you scale. Apollo is another option worth evaluating for teams that want prospecting and sequencing in one tool at a lower entry price point.
5

SDR / AE Training and Coaching  Recommended: Second Nature

SN

Second Nature

Second Nature is an AI-powered sales coaching platform built for rep onboarding and ongoing skills development. Reps practice real-world sales conversations with an AI persona covering cold call openers, discovery calls, objection handling, and demo pitches, then get scored on talk tracks, filler words, pacing, and playbook adherence. Managers see rep-by-rep readiness scores without having to shadow every call. Cuts ramp time for new SDRs and AEs by replacing passive training with active repetition at scale. It also holds up well beyond onboarding: when you launch a new product, pick up new competitive intelligence, or start hearing a new objection in the field, you can build a targeted scenario and get the whole team repped up quickly without waiting for the next QBR or all-hands.
